## Core Concepts

### Decision Mediation Layer (DML)

Explains how decision-making authority shifts from humans to AI systems through mediation, recommendation, filtering, and structured decision environments.

### AI-to-AI (A2A)

Describes a system state where AI agents and AI systems interact, negotiate, evaluate, and transact with each other autonomously.

### Pre-Decision Structuring (PDS)

Refers to how decision spaces are shaped before explicit human choices are made.

### Decision Authority Economy (DAE)

Describes the macro-level economic transition in which decision influence increasingly shifts from users toward AI-mediated systems.

### Attribution Collapse

Describes the degradation of traditional attribution systems in AI-mediated discovery and transaction environments.

### Judgment as a Service (JaaS)

Structured judgment systems that provide evaluative reasoning rather than simple information retrieval.

## Why This Matters

As decision-making authority increasingly shifts from humans toward AI systems, traditional discovery models, attribution systems, economic coordination structures, and transaction environments are being fundamentally reshaped.

This framework explores the emergence of AI-mediated decision environments and the structural consequences of AI-native interaction systems.
